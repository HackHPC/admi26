About HackHPC@ADMI26

HackHPC@ADMI26 is a five-day virtual hackathon focused on deconstructing the architecture of human knowledge. Using Wikipedia as a massive knowledge graph, teams will leverage advanced computational techniques to analyze the past, predict the future, and simulate alternative realities.

The event follows a collaborative model of project development, iterative feedback, and afternoon troubleshooting support to keep teams on track. Participants will move beyond "black box" solutions, building deep, graph-based applications that uncover the "how" and "why" behind the information we consume.

Innovation Tracks

Teams will focus their projects on one of three core technical pillars:

  • Where Did It Start? (Origin Tracking): Develop systems that backtrack through linked Wikipedia nodes to identify the most likely historical and conceptual origins of any topic.
  • What's Next? (Future Event Predictor): Analyze historical evolution patterns to predict upcoming developments, emerging keywords, and likely breakthroughs in converging fields.
  • What If? (Alternative Timeline Analyzer): Use counterfactual reasoning to identify missing prerequisites and "blocking factors" that prevent desired outcomes, such as global climate stability.

Key Technologies

Participants will apply a powerful stack of computational tools explored throughout the event:

  • Graph Theory & GNNs: Mapping entities as nodes and edges to understand how information spreads through neighborhoods.
  • Text Analysis & NLP: Utilizing TF-IDF and word embeddings to understand conceptual relationships and semantic similarity.
  • Causal Inference: Distinguishing between mere correlation and true causal chains to identify critical intervention points in history.
  • LLM Augmentation: Partnering with Large Language Models for hypothesis generation and semantic analysis.

Logistics at a Glance

  • Who: Students, researchers, and early-career technologists forming teams to build AI-enabled science gateway solutions.
  • What: A structured, virtual hackathon with kickoff sessions, team formation, project planning, and a final awards showcase.
  • When: June 22–26, 2026.
  • Where: Fully virtual — join via Zoom and collaborate using shared online workspaces.
