Judging Criteria

Teams are evaluated across three award areas plus a deliverables completion item, for a total of 100 base points. An additional +5 bonus points are awarded for confirmed Science Gateways poster submission, making the maximum possible score 105 points.

View Judging Criteria
25 pts Knowledge Graph Analytics Quality of analysis, use of computational methods, insightfulness of findings, evidence & justification, and alignment with the hackathon theme.
25 pts Knowledge Portal Usability & navigation, clarity of visualizations, presentation of knowledge relationships, design & accessibility, and integration of project results.
25 pts Gateways Impact Overall project impact, creativity & innovation, technical execution, communication & final presentation, and completeness of integration.
25 pts All Deliverables Completed GitHub repository, project poster, final presentation, and website/visualization/application all submitted by the deadline.
+5 pts Gateways Poster Bonus Confirmed submission of your project poster to the Science Gateways 2026 conference earns an automatic bonus added after judging scores are averaged.

Science Gateways 2026 Poster Guide

Creating your team project poster fulfills a mandatory HackHPC deliverable, and officially submitting it to the Science Gateways 2026 (Gateways 26) conference earns your team an automatic 5-point bonus toward your final judging score.

Gateways 2026 Conference Poster Template Submit Your Poster to Gateways 26
View Poster Guide

1. Hackathon Deliverable Requirements

For the hackathon itself, your team must produce a visual poster summarizing your project.

  • Format: The final poster must be submitted as a PDF file and added to your team's GitHub repository.
  • Bonus Proof: To get the +5 bonus points, you must provide evidence (e.g., an email confirmation) that your poster was successfully submitted to the Gateways 2026 conference.

2. Gateways 26 Content & Abstract Requirements

To submit your poster to Gateways 26, you will need to prepare an abstract to be submitted via the EasyChair portal.

  • Abstract Length & Format: The abstract must be 500 words or less and should be pasted directly into the submission portal form. Do not include references in this abstract text.
  • Required Abstract Details: The abstract must describe your ongoing work/early-stage ideas, the impact it has on your user community, and any lessons you have learned.
  • Content Guidelines — your work should address:
    • Problem & Solution Impact: What is the impact of the problem, and why are Science Gateways needed to solve it?
    • Compelling Nature: Why is your work notable and appealing to a broader audience?
    • Solution Architecture: Detail the specific tools, code, and technology stack your team used and why.
    • Outcomes: Share your achieved results or benchmarks.
    • Expanded Access: Explain how your project promotes accessibility, sustainability, and reproducibility.

3. Student-Led Designation

Gateways 26 features specific awards for student poster presentations, which are judged by peers.

  • Ensure you check the "student-led submission" option when applying in EasyChair.
  • To qualify, the first author must be an enrolled student who is responsible for the majority of the work (there are no special requirements for the remaining authors).

4. Generative AI Policy

Because your projects rely heavily on AI, it is highly important to follow the conference's AI policy.

  • You are permitted to use generative AI tools to prepare your manuscript, provided it does not result in false or plagiarized content.
  • Mandatory Disclosure: You must disclose your use of generative AI within the "Acknowledgments" or "Appendix" section of your work.

5. Physical Poster Specifications (For the Conference)

If accepted, you will need to bring a physical, printed poster to the event in Washington, D.C.

  • Dimensions: The physical poster must be a maximum width of 30 inches and a maximum length of 45 inches.
  • Note: You will be sharing a bulletin board with one other poster presenter, so strict adherence to these maximum dimensions is required so your poster does not overlap with theirs.

6. Key Deadlines & Event Details

Submission Deadline July 20, 2026 Submit abstract via EasyChair
Acceptance Notification Early August 2026 Decisions sent to submitting author
Poster Session September 23, 2026 Conference opening reception — at least one author must attend in person
